What is DeFi?

In centralized finance, your money is held by banks, corporations whose overarching goal is to make money. The financial system is full of third parties who facilitate money movement between parties, with each one charging fees for using their services. Good examples are your creditcard company and bank. Using their services requires you to pay fees to do so.

DeFi or "Decentralized finance" is the use of blockchain technology to disrupt these financial intermediaries. With DeFi, you can do most of the things that banks support: earn interest, borrow, lend, buy insurance, trade assets and more. But it's faster and doesn't require paperwork or a third party.

DeFi takes the basic premise of Bitcoin (digital money) and expands on it, creating an entire digital alternative to Wall Street, but without all the associated costs (think office towers, trading floors, banker salaries). This has the potential to create more open, free, and fair financial markets that are accessible to anyone with an internet connection. AMAZING!

- Open to anyone, just create a wallet
- Does not require any personal information
- Move assets freely without 3rd party permission or fees
- Higher interest rates compared to Wall Street

Users typically engage with DeFi via software called dapps (“decentralized apps”), most of which currently run on the Ethereum blockchain. Unlike a conventional bank, there is no application to fill out or account to open.
