Best Practices

Every crypto investor's biggest concern is the safety of their funds.
A new post appears daily about someone who lost their holdings due to various reasons. Today we're covering some solid tips to protect your cryptocurrency.

Preferably store seed phrases and other sensitive data offline. Use a flashdrive with a text file on it that keeps track of your wallets and passwords. This covers the worst case scenario of your system crashing, and you losing all your data. Keep a backup of these files on your computer. Stored somewhere hidden away, named obsecurely in case of a hacker searching for sensitive keywords on your system. A cold storage wallet eliminates these problems entirely.

To keep your crypto secure, it's recommended to utilize wallets and platforms that require at least two-factor authentication. This means that besides a password, a required code is send to your email, phone or google authenticator.
Not only should you be using multi-factor authentication for logging in, it can also be useful for the safety of your crypto transactions.

Never hold all assets in one wallet. Spreading your tokens across multiple wallets decreases the risk of an intruder seizing all of your belongings at once.

Don't sign into any 3rd party service using the wallets you hold your tokens on. Instead create a new wallet, send the required funds to the new wallet and proceed with your actions. After completion send the funds back to the holders wallet. You just prevented your wallets from unwanted exposure.
