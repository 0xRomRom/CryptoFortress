Decentralization Explained

In blockchain you often hear the term 'decentralization' but what does it mean?
In simple terms: It refers to the type of network, and more importantly the way it is managed.

Traditional networks are centralized, typically build around a single server which handles all
data processing & storage. Though beneficial in certain aspects, it also makes it for a single point of failure that can be exploited by hackers. The decision making is done by a set group of people.

+ Simple
+ Affordable Maintenance
+ Consistency

- Increased Downtime
- Higher Security Risks
- Limited Scalability.

By contrast, a decentralized network spreads the workload of this data processing & storage across multiple devices instead of relying on that single centralized server.

Every contributor to the network, also known as a 'node' interacts independently with other nodes in the network. Even when a master node crashes, the other smaller nodes can keep providing data to the network with limited or no disruption. Comparing this to the single point of failure from the centralized network, we can already notice improvements.

Over the years personal computers have become more powerful. All this computing power can be synced up and leveraged for distributed processing.

+ Increased Flex/Scalability
+ Better Performance
+ Enhanced Privacy

- Higher Maintainance Costs
- Not Suitable For All Business Models
