What is ICO/IDO?

ICO
ICO is a term you might hear often in crypto.
It's a shorthand for "Initial Coin Offering".
An initial coin offering is the crypto industry's equivalent to an "Initial Public Offering (IPO)", known from the traditional stock market.

A company seeking to raise money to create a new coin, app, or service can launch an ICO as a way to raise funds. Investors can buy into an ICO to receive a new cryptocurrency issued by the project. The token may have utility related to the product or service that the company is offering.

Some ICO's have yielded massive returns for investors. While others performed extremely poorly or simply exit scammed.
ICOs are, for the most part, completely unregulated. So investors must exercise a high degree of caution and diligence when researching and investing in ICOs.

IDO
An IDO uses a decentralized exchange (DEX) to facilitate the token sale. A crypto project provides their tokens to the DEX, users commit their funds through the platform, and the DEX completes the final distribution and transfer.
These processes are automated and occur via smart contracts on the blockchain.

The key difference here in ICO vs IDO is: ICO tokens are sold before an exchange listing. Where as IDO tokens are immediately listed on the DEX through which they're launched, gauranteing a solid exchange listing.
