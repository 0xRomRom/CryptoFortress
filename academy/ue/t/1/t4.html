Exchanges Basics

A crypto currency exchange is a place where retail traders get access to crypto markets. It allows them to buy crypto with fiat (USD, EUR, JPY etc.), or sell crypto for fiat. The exchange also exposes the user to all kinds of altcoins.

Depending on your country's law, you have access to variety of exchanges.
Top tier exchanges require new users to complete a KYC process upon registration. KYC stands for "know your customer".
Legal documents and a billing address are typically required in order to complete your KYC and account registration.
Exchanges are legally required to collect KYC data in case of law inforcement purposes.

After registration users are free to use the trading platform. You can go ahead and deposit, in order to buy crypto. Or transfer existing crypto funds onto the exchange, to trade the exchange's specific altcoin pairs.

Your personal account will have a variety of wallets to it's name. Every crypto currency supported on the platform will generate it's own wallet.
Let's say you plan on purchasing Solana coin. You proceed to the Solana trading page of the exchange, and purchase 10 Solana coins. The 10 Solana will appear in your exchange specific Solana wallet. This is the same wallet address you can use in order to receive Solana coin from outside of the exchange.

The same goes for any other supported crypto currency on the exchange. If your friend wants to send you some Ethereum. You proceed to the wallets page of your exchange. Look for the Ethereum wallet, and send your friend it's address.
Easy as that.
